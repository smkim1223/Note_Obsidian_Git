\subsection*{2.1 Enolate formation as rate-determining step}

If $\mathrm{C}_{3}$ enolate formation, $\left(\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{5} \mathrm{O}-\mathrm{H}\right) *_{M-O}$ is assumed to be the rate-determining step (RDS) [14-16], then, according to transition-state theory, the total rate of reaction can be described by the net rate of reaction for step 2 (S.6):
$$
\begin{equation*}
r_{\text {enol }}=r_{2}-r_{-2} \tag{S.19}
\end{equation*}
$$
where $r_{2}$ and $r_{-2}$ are the forward and reverse rates of enolate formation, respectively. These rates can be written as the products of their respective barrier crossing coefficients ( $v^{\ddagger}$ ) and transition state concentrations ( $c^{\ddagger}$ ) [17]:
$$
\begin{equation*}
r_{\text {enol }}=v^{\ddagger} c^{\ddagger}-v_{-}^{\ddagger} c_{-}^{\ddagger} \tag{S.20}
\end{equation*}
$$

Equilibrium relations for transition state formation in the forward and reverse reactions, based on microscopic equilibrium, are expressed in terms of thermodynamic activities ( $a_{i}$ ) as:
$$
\begin{equation*}
K_{2}^{\ddagger}=\frac{a^{\ddagger}}{a_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right) * M-O}} \tag{S.21}
\end{equation*}
$$
$$
\begin{equation*}
K_{-2}^{\ddagger}=\frac{a^{\ddagger}}{a_{\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) * M-O}} \tag{S.22}
\end{equation*}
$$
with forward and reverse rate constants:
$$
\begin{equation*}
k_{2}=v^{\ddagger} K_{2}^{\ddagger} \tag{S.23}
\end{equation*}
$$
$$
\begin{equation*}
k_{-2}=v_{-}^{\ddagger} K_{-2}^{\ddagger} \tag{S.24}
\end{equation*}
$$

Activities can be written as the product of their activity coefficients ( $\gamma_{i}$ ) and molar concentrations ( $c_{i}$ for solution phase components, $[L]$ for the total number of active sites), normalized by the standard molar concentration ( $c^{0}=1 \mathrm{~mol} \mathrm{~m}^{-3}$ ). Substituting equations (S.21-24) into equation (S.20) with step 1 (S.5) being quasi-equilibrated $\left(K_{1}=\frac{a_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right)^{*} M-O}}{a_{C_{3} H_{6} O a_{*} M-O}}\right)$ gives the enolate formation rate as:
$r_{\text {enol }}=\frac{k_{2} K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} o} a_{*}{ }_{M-O}}{\gamma^{\ddagger}}(1-\eta)[L]$
(S.25)
where $\eta$ is the approach to equilibrium, defined as:
$$
\begin{equation*}
\eta=\frac{a_{\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) *}{ }_{M-O}}{K_{1} K_{2} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O}} \tag{S.26}
\end{equation*}
$$

The following site balance, assuming negligible coverages of intermediates after the RDS, includes vacant Lewis acid-base site pairs ( $*_{M-O}$ ) and $\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}$ adsorbed at a M-O site $\left(\left(C_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}\right) *_{M-O}\right)$ :
$$
\begin{equation*}
[L]=c_{*_{M-O}}+c_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right) *_{M-O}} \tag{S.27}
\end{equation*}
$$
which can be expressed in terms of $a_{i}, \gamma_{i}$, and $K_{i}$ as:
$$
\begin{equation*}
1=\frac{a_{*_{M-O}}}{\gamma_{*}{ }_{M-O}}+\frac{K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} a_{*_{M-O}}}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right){ }_{M-O}}}=\frac{a_{*_{M-O}}}{\gamma_{*_{M-O}}}\left(1+\frac{\gamma_{*_{M-O}} K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O}}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right){ }^{*}{ }_{M-O}}}\right) \tag{S.28}
\end{equation*}
$$

Equation (S.28) can be substituted into equation (S.25) and, by assuming that $\eta \rightarrow$ 0 at low $C_{3}$ conversion, can be rewritten as follows to provide the general rate expression for enolate formation:
$$
\begin{equation*}
\frac{r_{\text {enol }}}{[L]}=\frac{\gamma_{*_{M-O}} k_{2} K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O}}{\gamma^{\ddagger}\left(1+\frac{\gamma_{*_{M-O}}}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right){ }_{M-O}}} K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O}\right)} \tag{S.29}
\end{equation*}
$$

For the case where the most abundant reactive intermediate (MARI) is a vacant Lewis acid-base site pair ( $*_{M-O}$ ) at low $\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right) *_{M-O}$ activity (i.e., $K_{1} \ll 1$ ), the rate of enolate formation simplifies to the following first-order expression:
$$
\begin{equation*}
\frac{r_{\text {enol }}}{[L]}=\frac{\gamma_{* M-O}}{\gamma^{\ddagger}} k_{2} K_{1} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} \tag{S.30}
\end{equation*}
$$

However, if $\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}$ adsorbed at a M-O site $\left(\left(\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}\right) *_{M-O}\right)$ becomes the MARI at higher surface coverages (i.e., $K_{1} \gg 1$ ), then the rate of enolate formation will transition to a zeroorder regime:
$$
\begin{equation*}
\frac{r_{\text {enol }}}{[L]}=\frac{\gamma_{*_{M-O}}}{\gamma^{\ddagger}} k_{2} \tag{S.31}
\end{equation*}
$$

\subsection*{2.2 Kinetically relevant C-C coupling step for propanal self-condensation}

By assuming that the kinetically relevant $\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{C}$ coupling step for $\mathrm{C}_{3}$ self-condensation is nucleophilic attack of the $\mathrm{C}_{3}$ enolate $\left(\left(\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{5} \mathrm{O}-\mathrm{H}\right) *_{M-O}\right)$ on a second $\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}$ adsorbed at the M-O site $\left(\left(\mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{6} \mathrm{O}\right) *_{M-O}\right)$ and all other steps are quasi-equilibrated, the rate expression can be written as:
$$
\begin{equation*}
r_{\text {self }}=k_{4} K_{3} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} a_{\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) * M-O}(1-\eta)[L] \tag{S.32}
\end{equation*}
$$

The total number of active sites, assuming negligible coverages of intermediates after C-C coupling, can be expressed as follows:
$$
\begin{equation*}
[L]=c_{*_{M-O}}+c_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right) *_{M-O}}+c_{\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) *_{M-O}}+c_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right)\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) *_{M-O}}+c_{\left(C_{7} H_{6} O\right)\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) *_{M-O}} \tag{S.33}
\end{equation*}
$$
which can be expressed in terms of $a_{i}, \gamma_{i}$, and $K_{i}$ as:
$$
\begin{equation*}
1=\frac{a_{* M-O}}{\gamma_{* M-O}}\left(1+\frac{\gamma_{* M-O} K_{1} a a_{3} H_{6} O}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right)^{*} M-O}}+\frac{\gamma_{* M-O} K_{1} K_{2} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} O}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right)^{*} M-O}}+\frac{\gamma_{* M-O} K_{1} K_{2} K_{3} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} O^{2}}{\gamma_{\left(C_{3} H_{6} O\right)\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) * M-O}}+\frac{\gamma_{* M-O} K_{1} K_{2} K_{9} a_{C_{3} H_{6} O} a_{C_{7} H_{6} O}}{\gamma_{\left(C_{7} H_{6} O\right)\left(C_{3} H_{5} O-H\right) * M-O}}\right) \tag{S.34}
\end{equation*}

\end{equation*}
